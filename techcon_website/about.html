History

It began in Lagos, Nigeria, in 2012. A small group of software engineers, designers, and tech entrepreneurs gathered in the back room of a modest co-working space in Yaba—what was then just beginning to earn its name as “Yabacon Valley.”

Frustrated by the lack of platforms for pan-African tech collaboration, they asked a bold question: What if there was one event where African talent from every corner of the continent could meet, share, and build the future together?

The first edition was grassroots—just 80 participants, unreliable power supply, and a projector that overheated every hour. But the atmosphere was electric. Among the early attendees was Ayo Kendi, a self-taught developer from Ibadan. His prototype for a solar-powered IoT water sensor drew the attention of investors from Nairobi and Cape Town. Today, Ayo runs a clean-tech startup operating across East and West Africa.

From there, TechCon grew rapidly. By 2016, it had become a travelling conference, rotating across African cities—Accra, Kigali, Nairobi, Dakar— building a shared tech identity across borders and languages.

Today, TechCon is more than a conference. It's a celebration of homegrown brilliance, a proving ground for big ideas, and a powerful reminder that the future of technology is being co-created across the continent.

Mission

TechCon exists to empower African innovation, foster collaboration across borders, and amplify voices shaping the continent’s digital future. We believe technology should be inclusive, sustainable, and locally relevant.

Past Speakers

Dr. Amina Clarke

A data ethics pioneer and professor at the University of Nairobi, Dr. Clarke’s keynote on “Decolonizing AI” inspired a movement toward more equitable machine learning practices across Africa.

Chinedu Eke

A fintech innovator from Lagos, Chinedu introduced a blockchain-powered remittance system during TechCon 2019 that now supports rural banking in five countries.

Zuri Mbeki

As co-founder of an edtech platform serving low-income communities in South Africa, Zuri’s 2021 panel helped shape continental policy on digital inclusion.
